Consultancy services in ICT

Nitram provides consulting services to enterprises, advising them on the most suitable business process management software to adopt. This includes recommending Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems, which allow organizations to utilize a unified set of integrated applications to manage their business and automate numerous back-office functions related to technology, services, and human resources. Expenditure verification/Spending Assessment

Expenditure Verification is an engagement to perform agreed-upon procedures regarding the Financial Report for a Grant Contract. The objective is to submit a report of factual findings to the Grantor about the specific verification procedures performed. We examine the information in the Beneficiary's Financial Report and compare it with the terms and conditions of the Grant Contract. The Contracting Authority then assesses the factual findings we reported and draws its conclusions. The Spending Assessment approach to resource tracking is a comprehensive and systematic methodology to determine the flow of resources from the source to the point of service delivery. This helps identify bottlenecks in resource flows and informs improved resource mobilization, allocation, and utilization. It also provides insights into the extent of harmonization and alignment between the resource envelope and the programmatic priorities. Careful consideration of the scope is critical to ensure timely delivery and quality of the products. The primary objective of the Spending Assessment is to strengthen the comprehensive tracking of resource spending, leveraging both technical and financial resources for the implementation, management, monitoring, and evaluation of donor-funded projects.

Delivery Capability Assessment

Nitram supports Development Partners and Donors in conducting Delivery Capability Assessments (DCA) on implementing partners before awarding grants and financial aid. Before providing funding, it is essential to carry out a qualitative assessment of the key financial and administrative policies, procedures, and practices of the implementing partners. This ensures that Donors can effectively manage their fiduciary risk. It is also important to undertake audits during the lifetime of each grant, as well as at the end, to verify that resources have been used properly and in full compliance with the grant agreement. This applies to a range of implementing partners, including Civil Society Organizations (CSOs), Private Sector Organizations (PSOs), International non-governmental organizations, Government Ministries, Departments, and Agencies For-profit organizations
